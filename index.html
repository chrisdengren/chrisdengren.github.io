I am an incoming Ph.D. student in Applied Physics at Harvard University, joining the 2026 cohort as an Ashford Fellow. I graduated from Columbia University in 2026 with a B.A. in Physics and Applied Mathematics.

My research sits at the intersection of physics, instrumentation, and computation. I've worked on Monte Carlo simulations of electron scattering for multicolor electron microscopy in the Prigozhin Lab, humidity-driven actuators for evaporation-based energy harvesting in the Sahin Lab, and X-ray spectroscopy of magnetic white dwarf binaries in the High-Energy Astrophysics Group at Columbia Astrophysics Lab.

Outside of physics, I play badminton and chess, hike, and draw anime characters on RedNote/Xiaohongshu.
