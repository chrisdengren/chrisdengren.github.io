A corner of the site for physics that doesn't fit on a CV. Everything below is interactive — poke at it. Built in plain JavaScript on HTML canvas, no libraries.

A quantum wavepacket under your cursor

A Gaussian wavepacket moving rightward, evolving under the time-dependent Schrödinger equation. Move your mouse to sculpt a potential barrier — mouse x is its position, and y controls its height (up is taller). Watch the wave partly tunnel, partly reflect.

hover the canvas · barrier follows your cursor

Double pendulum

Two rigid rods, four state variables, and no closed-form solution. Integrated with RK4. Initial angles are slightly randomized each drop — so two "identical" runs diverge, which is the whole point.

each drop finds a different future

Monte Carlo estimates π

Throw darts uniformly at a unit square. The fraction that land inside the quarter circle, times 4, converges to π. Slowly. This is the simplest Monte Carlo estimator I know — and a gentle ancestor of the MC simulations I've run for electron scattering.
